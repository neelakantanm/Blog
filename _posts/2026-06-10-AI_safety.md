In the past few years, and perhaps especially in the past year, “AI” may have become one of the most commonly used initialisms in the world. Nevertheless, I think the vast majority of people who use the “word” AI do not really know what they mean by it. From conversations with people around me, I have realized that many seem to think of AI as a single superintelligence existing somewhere on the internet, capable of automating most human intellectual tasks. I have also observed that there is a great deal of unjustified stigma around AI. Unless someone is familiar with the current technology industry or comes from academia, I do not think they usually know what an LLM is. In some AI has become a meme, which reminds me of the origin of the word meme itself. Richard Dawking coined the word "meme" in his book *The Selfish Gene (1976)* as cultural ideas, behaviors, catchphrases, or melodies that spread from person to person through imitation and evolve over time via natural selection—similar to biological genes.
